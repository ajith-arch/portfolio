The Problem

Why telehealth fails ‚Äî repeatedly

Through research, I identified three systemic problems that cause telehealth experiences to fail:

01
üß†

Missing or Unstructured Context

Patients forget timelines, medications, or key history. Doctors spend the first minutes extracting basics instead of providing care.

Impact: Longer consults, repeated questions, patient frustration
02
üìÅ

Fragmented Health Memory

Chat-based help is not reusable. Older issues and reports are hard to retrieve and share during a consult. Every visit starts from scratch.

Impact: Lost context, redundant tests, poor continuity of care
03
üó£Ô∏è

Language Mismatch in Consults

Many doctors prefer English while many patients prefer regional languages. This creates confusion, longer consults, and poor adherence to care instructions.

Impact: Miscommunication, missed instructions, lower trust

Design Goal

Create an AI-powered care flow that helps patients communicate faster, converts chats into reusable health records, generates doctor-ready briefs, and supports India's multilingual reality ‚Äî all while prioritizing safety, consent, and trust.

Core Innovation

The Four Pillars of Care AI

Pillar 1

Not chat logs ‚Äî structured, reusable health records

Instead of raw conversation history, each interaction becomes a structured "Case" that users can return to anytime. This turns Care AI from a one-off chatbot into a longitudinal care layer.

Pillar 3

Doctor Summary Pack

Patient-approved, scannable in 30 seconds

Before booking or joining a consult, Care AI generates a one-page doctor-ready brief. The user must review, edit, and approve before sharing ‚Äî nothing is auto-shared.

Summary Pack Contents
1 Chief complaint
2 Symptom timeline
3 Relevant history (conditions, allergies)
4 Current medications
5 Attachments with extracted highlights
6 Patient's top 3 questions
7 Red-flag screening outcomes

Safety, Trust & AI

Responsible AI design

Healthcare AI requires careful boundaries. I designed clear guardrails for what AI can and cannot do, with privacy and consent at the core.

‚úì AI Can

  • Ask structured questions to understand symptoms
  • Summarize user input into a scannable brief
  • Recommend specialty and suggest next steps
  • Highlight urgency signals and red flags
  • Translate speech and generate transcripts

‚úó AI Cannot

  • Confirm a diagnosis
  • Replace emergency services
  • Modify medications without clinician review
  • Guarantee translation accuracy in low-confidence situations
  • Auto-share information without patient consent

Reflection

What I learned

Designing for healthcare AI taught me about balancing automation with trust, and complexity with clarity.

01

Longitudinal > Transactional

Converting chats into reusable "Cases" changes the relationship from one-off help to ongoing care partnership.

02

Context is Clinical Value

Structured patient context saves doctors time and improves care quality. Design should capture, not just display.

03

Multilingual ‚â† Translation

True multilingual support means designing for language switching, uncertainty, and cultural context ‚Äî not just word replacement.

04

Transparency Builds Trust

Explaining "why" the AI asks questions and showing confidence levels makes users more comfortable sharing sensitive information.
