On sprawling campuses, even simple item transfers become 40-minute commitments. Students skip returning library books, delay lab equipment exchanges, and miss department submissions‚Äînot from negligence, but from logistics friction.

The Design Challenge

Multi-receiver deliveries break everything

The initial design supported sending items to multiple receivers. It seemed like a feature win‚Äîuntil we hit the coordination wall.

‚è≥

Sequential Acceptance

Each receiver must accept before the robot proceeds. One delayed acceptance blocks the entire delivery chain.

üö´

Sudden Cancellations

A receiver canceling mid-route forces complex rerouting logic. The robot might be closer to receiver #3 but waiting for #2.

ü§ñ

Robot Idle Time

Robots waiting for acceptance can't serve other requests. One unresponsive receiver reduces fleet efficiency.

üò§

Sender Frustration

Senders have no control after dispatch. They watch the tracking screen, hoping each receiver responds.

Core Problem

Individual receivers are unpredictable. Departments are not. Libraries, labs, and offices have fixed hours and dedicated staff. Shifting from person-to-person to person-to-department removes the coordination bottleneck.

Design Evolution

Instead of designing around the multi-receiver problem, I redesigned the model to eliminate it.

A mobile-first experience that lets any verified campus user send items to people or departments, with autonomous robots handling logistics.

Reflection

What I learned

The best solutions often come from reframing the problem, not engineering around it.

01

Pivot, don't patch

When multi-receiver created cascading problems, the answer wasn't more complex logic. It was changing the model entirely.

02

Constraints reveal opportunities

Campus-only felt limiting. But it enabled pre-acceptance‚Äîsomething that wouldn't work in an open network.

03

Institutions > individuals

Individual behavior is unpredictable. Institutional behavior is not. Libraries will be open during posted hours.

04

Show confidence before action

The pre-approval badge builds confidence before selection. Users know their delivery won't wait.
